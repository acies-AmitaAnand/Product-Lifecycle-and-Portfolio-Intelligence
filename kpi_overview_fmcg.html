Commercial value
Net Sales (Revenue)
direct
net_sales = sum(units_sold × selling_price)
Aggregated per SKU from transaction rows. Backbone of all value scoring.
❯ Does revenue reflect returns or discounts, or is it gross? Does promo pricing suppress it?
Gross Margin
model-derived
gross_margin = net_sales − (units_sold × purchase_cost)
Uses mean purchase cost as a proxy — cost is assumed stable across the period.
❯ If cost fluctuates seasonally or by supplier, mean cost will overstate margin on peak months.
Revenue Growth
model-derived
(rev_2023 − rev_2022) / rev_2022 → fill 0 if missing
YoY comparison. Missing year data is zeroed — treated as flat, not unknown.
❯ SKUs launched in 2023 get growth=0, masking true acceleration. How many new SKUs are in the set?
Demand Stability (CV)
model-derived
CV = std(monthly_units) / mean(monthly_units)
Stability = 1 − normalized CV
Higher stability = more predictable demand. Used to reward consistent performers in value score.
❯ CV ignores trend direction — a fast-growing SKU looks "unstable" even if the signal is positive.
Operational complexity
Lead Time
direct
lead_time_days (avg per SKU from dataset)
Directly available. Used as a complexity driver and safety stock input.
❯ Is this supplier lead time or total replenishment cycle (including internal handling)?
Safety Stock Proxy
model-derived
safety_stock = lead_time_days × demand_std
Approximation — no actual stock-on-hand data. demand_std from monthly sales variation.
❯ Actual safety stock decisions may include service-level targets. Is there real inventory data available?
Stockout Frequency
direct
sum(stock_out_flag) per SKU
Count of transaction rows where stockout was flagged. High counts = supply chain fragility.
❯ Does the flag capture full-day stockouts or just moments? Are partial stockouts included?
Promo Dependency
direct
% of revenue rows where promo_flag = 1
High dependency = SKU needs discounting to sell. Signals weak organic demand.
❯ Is promo_flag capturing trade promotions, markdowns, or both? Are some promos margin-neutral?
Volatility Class
model-derived
CV < 0.2 → Stable | < 0.5 → Variable | else → Unstable
Mapped: 0.3 / 0.6 / 1.0 in complexity score
Discretized CV. Used as numeric weight in operational complexity formula.
❯ Thresholds (0.2 / 0.5) are arbitrary. Sensitivity to these cut-offs is untested — no SKU hit "Unstable".
Composite scores
Commercial Value Score
model-derived
= norm(revenue) + norm(margin) + norm(growth) + norm(stability)
All components equally weighted
Composite 0–1 score used to place SKUs in the value-complexity quadrant.
❯ Equal weighting assumes revenue = margin = growth = stability. Should margin carry more weight?
Operational Complexity Score
model-derived
= norm(lead_time) + norm(supplier_dep) + norm(promo_dep)
+ norm(stockout) + norm(volatility_class)
Five components summed with equal weight. Drives the rationalize/keep/grow classification.
❯ Stockout and volatility may be correlated — are they being double-counted as complexity signals?
Portfolio Complexity Index
estimated
PCI = avg(SKU proliferation, supplier fragmentation,
avg volatility, low-velocity %, lead instability, promo %)
Enterprise-level score. Current value: 0.5509. Dominated by supplier fragmentation (1.20) and SKU proliferation (1.02).
❯ Baselines (100 SKUs, 50 suppliers) are hardcoded. Are these industry benchmarks or internal targets?
Cannibalization Risk Score
estimated
= max(0, −min_correlation)
between other_SKU promo_flag ↔ target SKU units_sold
Negative correlation of one SKU's promotion with another's sales. Highest risk: Beverages category.
❯ Correlation ≠ causation. Seasonal timing could create spurious negative correlations. How is this validated?
