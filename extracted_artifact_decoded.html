Tab 0

Portfolio Health Map

Monitor SKU value, complexity, and operational fragility across your portfolio.

1

Understand the KPI strip

8 headline metrics at the top. Green ▲ = healthy improvement. Red ▲ = rising risk (complexity, stockouts). Each card is interactive — hover to see calculation details.

2

Filter dynamically

Use the form to narrow by category, region, year, or minimum revenue. The entire dashboard recalculates instantly. Try Category: Beverages to see beverage-specific metrics.

3

Identify risk signals

Portfolio Complexity Index > 0.55 and Promo Dependency > 40% are red flags. High stockout counts indicate supply fragility.

4

Export insights

Click "Generate Report" to create a summary of current filters. Use this for weekly decks or stakeholder updates.

Value × Complexity Matrix

Interactive scatter plot. Scroll to zoom, drag to pan, hover for SKU detail.
1

The 80/20 principle

In most portfolios, ~20% of SKUs generate 80% of revenue. This chart shows exactly where that threshold sits for your portfolio.

2

Long tail analysis

The bottom 40% of SKUs often contribute < 10% revenue but carry full operational cost (stockouts, shelf space, supplier POs).

3

Adjust the threshold

Move the slider to change the revenue target %. The cumulative line and bar colors update instantly.

4

Sort and compare

Toggle sort order to see revenue distribution from different angles — high→low reveals winners, low→high reveals tail drag.

⚙️ Pareto Controls

Revenue Concentration (Pareto)

Bars = individual SKU revenue. Line = cumulative %. Hover for SKU details.
1

Three key metrics

Margin % (bars, left axis), Demand Volatility (line, right axis), Stockout Events (bars, secondary). Higher volatility = harder forecasting.

2

Channel comparison

Modern Trade and E-Commerce typically show higher margins but more volatility. General Trade is stable but lower margin.

3

Risk vs reward

High margin + high volatility = growth opportunity with supply risk. Low margin + low volatility = steady but commoditized.

Pie chart showing revenue contribution by channel. Hover for percentages.
1

Set criteria

Define complexity threshold and revenue floor. SKUs above complexity AND below revenue floor are rationalization candidates.

Model the impact

The sim calculates: revenue at risk, ops savings (from reduced complexity), stockout reduction, and new portfolio complexity index.

Demand transfer rate

Launch Readiness Tracker

Score new SKU launches across 5 dimensions — market fit, supply, margin, channel, and risk.

1

Fill the SKU brief

Enter projected revenue, margin, lead time, promo budget, supplier count, and distribution channels. Each input feeds into the readiness score.

2

Read the radar chart

Five axes: Market Fit, Supply Readiness, Margin Health, Channel Coverage, Risk Profile. A balanced pentagon = launch-ready.

3

Interpret the verdict

Score ≥75 = ✅ Launch Ready. 50–74 = ⚠️ Conditional (address gaps). < 50 = 🚫 Not Ready (significant risks).

4

Compare benchmarks

Grey overlay = category average. Your SKU should exceed benchmark on 3+ axes before launch.
