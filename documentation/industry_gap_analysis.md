The Long-Tail SKU Proliferation Trap** | Variant SKUs are added to capture niche consumer segments; companies fail to prune legacy items, creating "bad complexity" that locks up shelf space and manufacturing capacity. | • Sales uplift of up to 5% from the Simplify to Grow framework.<br>• More than 80% of consumers (85% in the US, 88% in Europe) plan to further reduce spend on fast-moving consumer goods.<br>• Retailer margins have dropped significantly: 15% in the US, 25% in Europe.<br>• Simplification prioritizing consumer needs breaks the negative complexity cycle. | Transition from simple tail-cutting to consumer-needs-led portfolio reviews. | **2. Supplier Network Over-Integration & Complexity** | Relying on heavily interconnected, universal supplier bases creates systemic vulnerability. Delisting single SKUs fails to reduce overhead because vendor relationships remain active for other products. | • 60% of companies have visibility into their Tier-1 suppliers — but only 30% can see beyond Tier-2 (down 7 pp from prior year).<br>• 90% of companies lack sufficient digital talent to meet their supply chain digitization goals (unchanged since 2020).<br>• Only 30% of boards have a deep understanding of supply chain risks.<br>• Supply chain disruptions lasting 1+ month occur every 3.7 years on average across industries. | Move from flat supplier structures to category-focused supplier clusters with dual-sourcing, digital twin scenario modeling, and subtier mapping to build network modularity and reduce blind spots. | [McKinsey: Global Supply Chain Leader Survey 2024](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations/our-insights/global-supply-chain-leader-survey-2024-reshaping-with-resilience) (October 2024) |
| **3. Promotional Margin Erosion & Dependency** | Commercial teams run deep promotions to hit volume targets in a low-inflation, cost-conscious environment, leading to margin erosion and training consumers to buy only on discount. | • ~50% of all promotional sales cannibalize non-promotional baseline sales.<br>• 80% of promotional investment fails to contribute to category growth.<br>• Only 1 in 10 brands simultaneously grows share, category leadership, and profit.<br>• ~75% of RGM programs do not generate positive profit growth for both retailer and manufacturer.<br>• More than 80% of CPG CEOs are dissatisfied with their RGM results despite multi-year investments. | Evolve pack-price architecture; implement AI-enabled RGM tools using a trifecta approach (consumer value + trade profit + 360° manufacturer ROI) to monitor pricing, dead net pricing, and localized elasticity. | [Bain & Company: Why Consumer Product Companies Need to Solve Revenue Growth Management](https://www.bain.com/how-we-help/why-consumer-product-companies-need-to-solve-revenue-growth-management/) (January 2022) |
| **4. Leverage AI-powered assortment optimization and private brand differentiation to align product mix with local consumer needs and cost-to-serve. | [Bain & Company: Top 5 for 2025 — Retailer Resolutions and Forecast for the New Year](https://www.bain.com/insights/retailer-resolutions-and-forecast-for-2025/) (January 2025) |

Use multi-echelon optimization models to release locked-up working capital without compromising service levels. | [McKinsey: Harnessing the Power of AI in Distribution Operations](https://www.mckinsey.com/industries/retail/our-insights/harnessing-the-power-of-ai-in-distribution-operations) (November 2024) |
